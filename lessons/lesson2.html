Lesson 2: Synaptic Transmission

When a neuron fires, it sends spikes to all connected neurons through synapses. These connections are the pathways that allow information to flow through the neural network.

Understanding Synapses

Synaptic Connections

In our visualization, you can see these as the grey lines connecting neurons. Each connection represents a synapse where spikes travel from one neuron to another.

Synaptic Weights

The strength of each connection, shown in the weight panels when you enable "Show Weights". Stronger connections (higher weights) have more influence on the receiving neuron.

Signal Propagation

When a neuron fires, its spike travels along all its outgoing connections. The receiving neurons add the connection weight to their membrane potential.

Dynamic Connection Activity

Watch for these visual cues:

  • Grey Connections: Inactive synapses (always visible)
  • Colored Connections: Active synapses carrying spikes
  • Cluster Colors: Connections light up in the color of the firing neuron cluster
  • Synchronous Activity: When neurons fire together, their connections brighten

Weight Information Panels

Enable "Show Weights" to see detailed information about each neuron:

  • Neuron ID: Unique identifier
  • Current Voltage: Real-time membrane potential
  • Connection List: All outgoing connections with their weights
  • Color Coding: Green (strong), Yellow (medium), Grey (weak)

Experimentation

Try these experiments:

  • Increase connection probability to see denser networks
  • Use "Inject Spike" to trigger cascading activity
  • Click on neurons to monitor their voltage traces
  • Watch how different clusters interact through their connections
